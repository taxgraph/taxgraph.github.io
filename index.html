About

The purpose of this site is to give tax filers a super simple visualization of how taxes work in the United States. The information is simply a better rendering of the official tax information, available here: IRS rate schedules for 2014, and US Tax Tables 2014.

I hope this site busts the following common myth:

"If I earn just a little more money, I end up with less after-tax income because I got bumped into a higher tax bracket."
This is simply impossible. The US uses a graduated tax rate, which means each income bracket tax rate is only applied on income amounts over that income bracket, not on all your monies! Read about this here: http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Income_tax_in_the_United_States#Marginal_tax_rates

Is it accurate? The above data conveys the tax rate schedule put out by the IRS. It does not cover all sorts of possible credits or deductions you might be eligible for. Such credits and deductions can only lower your owed tax further, so think of this site as providing a rough conservative estimate of what you owe.
